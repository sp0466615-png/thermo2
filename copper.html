About Litharge Oxide Plant

Litharge Oxide (Yellow Oxide), also known as Lead Monoxide, is produced from Lead Sub Oxide using a specially designed furnace system.

The raw material from the Barton Mill Plant is further processed in a Stainless Steel Furnace Pot where it reacts with natural oxygen at controlled temperatures.

This process results in the formation of high-quality Litharge Oxide, widely used in industrial applications.

Our plant is engineered using modern technology ensuring high efficiency, durability, and consistent output.

Working Process

The Lead Sub Oxide is charged into the furnace and heated to a specific temperature. A stainless steel stirrer continuously rotates inside the furnace to ensure proper mixing and reaction with oxygen.

The system is equipped with a heavy-duty Reduction Gear Box driven by a motor, ensuring smooth and maintenance-free operation over long periods.

This advanced mechanism ensures efficient oxidation and high-quality production of Litharge Oxide.
